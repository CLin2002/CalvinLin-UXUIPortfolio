Citymapper Feature Design

UI Design

Team


Calvin Lin - Concept Design, UI Design


Carissa - Concept Design, UI Design


Thomas - UI Design


Tools


Figma

Objective


Accommodate for travel time differences for group members with different starting points. Alleviate the stress of planning group departure times and ETA uncertainty.


Understanding The Problem


Planning a trip with a large group can be challenging and confusing. Differences in travel times can make it difficult to predict when members of the group will arrive. This issue becomes magnified when the uncertainty of transit schedules becomes involved.

Gathering Insights


Our first task was to gain insights into how users currently planned their travels. We found that most users prioritized the fastest, most efficient transit routes. Users of Google and Apple maps noted that ETAs are unreliable and often inaccurate, further explaining how these issues cause stress, especially when traveling to unfamiliar places such as new cities.

Proposing a New Feature


From the insights we gathered, one of the three main features that we planned on implementing is the Group ETA feature. My role was to plan and design the wireframe and wireflows. This new feature faced logistical errors initially, and we received valuable feedback about user privacy. Additionally, we identified that leaving a group required too many steps, and the new feature lacked clarity and visibility on the home page.

Usability Testing


During our think-aloud interviews, users expressed mixed feelings about the new feature. Some users found forming new groups for each trip cumbersome, while others felt the process of leaving groups was tedious and frustrating. One user stated, "I feel uneasy commuting alone, so I will let someone know once I get to my destination as a way to check-in." Another noted, "When traveling with friends, we would set a meet-up point during the day. Sometimes I would have to wait a while if I got there first." Overall, we found that users were more than happy to see this feature implemented in Citymapper, believing that the new feature would improve their transit experience.


Final Mockup


The final mockup introduced a more robust visual hierarchy for the Group ETA feature, greatly improving clarity. Although user privacy concerns were not initially raised, it was important for our team to present users with the option to opt out of sharing locations based on their discretion. To address the lengthy process of leaving a group, we incorporated the new feature on the main page of Citymapper.


Takeaways


Designing for an existing platform is difficult. The most difficult part of this project was to develop a feature that enhanced the existing user experience without compromising the original integrity of the app.


Privacy is important. Protecting user data and being transparent about information sharing should always be a top priority in design.


The feature should be easily accessible. Any valuable feature should be instantly recognizable and easily accessible. Users should not need to navigate through multiple screens to perform a task.
