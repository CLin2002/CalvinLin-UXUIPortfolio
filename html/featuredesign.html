Citymapper Feature Design

UI Design

Overview

Objective


Accommodate for travel time differences for group members with different starting points. Alleviate the stress of planning group departure times and ETA uncertainty.


Personal contributions


I contributed to this project by creating prototypes and mockups on Figma. Additionally, I was able to design a concept that we would later refine and showcase.

Tools


Figma

Initial Research


Citymapper is a well established app that aims to get users from point A to point B using different means of travel, with a particular focus on public transit systems. The goal was to dissect and understand how Citymapper uses different UI elements so that we were able to implement a new feature that was minimally invasive to the existing UI.


Taking a look at the UI and patterns, we noticed some inconsistencies, such as the use of different colours for icons that did not directly relate to traveling. Other features included saving and sharing specific routes, as well as features that allowed users to plan in advance (only applies to solo travel).


Potential areas of improvement that have been identified are the addition of more social features, trip planning, as well as more accommodations for tourism.






User interviews & persona creation


Diving deeper into understanding potential features that we could implement into Citymapper, we studied the mapping habbits of users. We conduected 5 Ask and Watch interviews, conducted interviews to understand overall transit experiences, observed users' current process with navigation apps, as well as conducting a Think Out Loud interview with users.


What we discovered are that users prioritized taking the fastest route with fewest transfers. Some concerns were raised regarding transit safety and poor weather. Frustrations users had were incorrect ETAs, frustration with overcrowded buses and trains, as well as figuring out how to navigate new cities.


From these discoveries, we created two user personas, a primary and a secondary user. Our primary user, Alanna wants to arrive at her destinations as soon as possible while minimizing her walking distances. Noah, the secondary user, a frequent traeller, wants to be able to explore new cities more efficiently while saving money.


Areas of focus


Through user interview and research, as well as our persona creation, we were able to narrow down our ideas. Firstly, we want to find a solution to reduce transit delays, as well as notify users of bus capacity. Next, we wanted to find a way to improve transit safety and conditions. Lastly, we wanted to help tourists explore new and unfamiliar cities.

Initial Concepts


Moving forward from the user research and persona creation, Carissa and I came up with three new features to implement. The first feature is Place of Interest, the second is Itinerary, finally, Group Locations. Each of these new features aims to address distinct issues brought up by our research.


Places of Interest


Places of Interest was a concept that Carissa and I designed, however, Thomas was in charge of designing the mockups and wireflow of Places of Interest. This feature highlights attractions and notable places near the user's location. The goal of this feature is to help tourists explroe unfamiliar cities, providing them with options and ideas of possible things to do during their trip. This works by displaying notable landmarks using the user's current destination.


Itinerary


Carissa was in charge of designing the concept, mockups and wireflow of Itinerary. Users can create itineraries with specific stops throughout the day. Additionally, users can decide to start specific tasks or plan the whole day with this new feature. The goal of this feature is to assist traveller plan out trips with multiple stops, allowing them to quickly adjust their travel plan during the day.


Group Locations


Group Locations was a concept that I design. I was also in charge of the mockups and wireflows involved in the creation of Group Locations. Group Locations displays the live locations of other groups members to a shared destination. Members of a group will have access to each others ETA along their commute. The purpose of this feature is to accommodate for travel time differences between group members, specifically to alleviate the stress involved in meticulously planning group departure times. This feature builds on top of Citymapper's existing destination sharing. However, the existing feature does not allow users to track the ETA and location of others. This new feature helps users better plan and time trips.


Usability


Both Places of Interest and Itinerary were deemed to lack uniqueness. Users noted that there are other solutions to these issuse that can be solved by other existing apps that already perform the same tasks better. Additionally, users noted that, adding such features would make Citymapper feel like Yelp. Finally, users stated that Group Locations could provide value for Citymapper, however, the flow for editing groups is too confusing and hard to access. Other concerns included "single-use" groups for one trip specifically.


Final Design


Taking into consideration the issues addressed by our usability test, the group redesigned aspects of the new proposed group feature. Users now have the option to hide their shared location, however, turning off location will not allow users to view other users location. The groups feature is now it's own feature, rather than a combined feature with the existing UI. This helps solve the issue with not being able to find the Group feautre.


Feature prototype


After refining and testing our idea, patching holes in logic and improving usability, our group was able to present a fully thought out prototype. This new feature was well received and notably had little to no system breaking errors or notable usability issues.
