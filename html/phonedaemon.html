Objective


Uncover the hidden processes in mobile devices known as phone daemons and identify how to confront the issue in a non-traditional and speculative manner. Design a speculative design solution to combat phone daemons.


Understanding The Problem


In computing, daemons are background processes that operate silently, performing specific tasks without requiring user interaction. Extending this metaphor to modern smartphones, phone daemons are invisible digital agents that monitor and influence user behaviour without explicit consent or awareness. However since the introduction of the smartphone, it has secretly changed the way we live our lives specifically with our daily social interactions.

Gathering Insights


To uncover the meaning behind phone daemons, our group conducted extensive background research on the topic. We explored technical definitions of phone daemons, as well as the implications of them. I focused on the biological aspect of how phone daemons and phone addiction can affect the biology of developing brains.

Q: How do phone daemons affect the psychology during development?

" A recent study scanned participants’ brains with a phone addiction and discovered a change in their brain’s grey matter. According to the researchers, the physical shape and size of their brains resembled that of drug users. "


Q: What impacts do phone daemons have?

" They cultivate this reliance by triggering the feeling of having to constantly stay updated in real-time, leading to an impulsive urge for phone checking. Reinforcing habits like mindless scrolling or binge-watching content also heightens dependency. "

Q: How do phone daemons affect users?

" Phone daemons encourage habitual phone checking, which disrupts conversations and shared activities during social gatherings. This leads to a phenomenon of being “alone together,” where individuals are physically present but mentally absorbed in their phones, making interactions feel shallow and disengaged.

Constraints


While we were excited to explore our possibilities, we had to ensure that our solution was not to ignore the existence of phone daemons, that we reimagine an alternative present where phone daemons are managed, and that we must consider the implication of our solutions. Based on our research and discussions, we wanted to explore physical interventions, a multi-staged intervention program, and phone licensing

Usability testing


The completion of the mockups led us to our usability testing phase. We conducted rigorous usability testing, employing methods such as the think-aloud method and A/B testing. This approached allowed us to gain valuable insights into user interactions and preferences.


Think-Aloud Method


During the think-aloud sessions, users were asked to vocalize their thoughts and actions while using the app. This method provided real-time feedback on their experiences and helped us identify user pain points and areas for improvement.


A/B Testing


In addition to think-aloud sessions, we conducted A/B testing to assess the impact of specific design elements, particularly the use of color within the system. Users were presented with two versions of certain screens, and their preferences and feedback were analyzed.

A/B Testing Insights


During A/B tests, users provided valuable feedback on the use of color within the app. While overall color did not significantly impact the user experience, users expressed a preference for yellow buttons related to monetary functions. Yellow was associated more strongly with money in users' minds compared to the color blue.


Iterative Design: Armed with these insights, we prioritized design improvements to address these issues in subsequent iterations. By enhancing transparency, optimizing screen size and interactivity, and refining the donation tracking system, we aimed to create a more user-friendly and intuitive app.

Compilation of research findings

Final Mockup


Incorporating user feedback from usability testing, I made critical adjustments to improve the user experience. The donation tracking was separated into active and completed donations, offering users clear insights into their donation status. The discovery page introduced a drop-down filter for enhanced usability. The cards on the discovery page were redesigned to be more linear, accommodating larger thumbnail images and more campaign information.

Takeaways


Quality over quantity. Users value quality campaigns that make a meaningful impact more than being overwhelmed by numerous options. Prioritizing user needs allowed us to create an outstanding user experience and product.


Respect what users’ say. Our research findings were invaluable to our project's success. Listening to users' feedback was crucial, and we considered it carefully throughout the design process.


Let users’ make their own decisions. Big ED's primary objective was to provide users with a positive experience and empower them to make informed decisions. We offered complete campaign breakdowns, allowing users to decide whether to donate.
