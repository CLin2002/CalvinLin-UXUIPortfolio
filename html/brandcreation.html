Glow In The Dough

Web Development

Overview

Objective


Design a brand concept and make it come to life using HTML/CSS/JavaScript.


Personal contributions


My contributions in this project included designing the style guide with my partner, completing the about us, contact, and checkout pages, as well as the landing page assets using Adobe Illustrator.

Team


Calvin Lin - Concept Design, Web Development, Asset Creation


Pocholo - Concept Design, Web Development


Tools


HTML/CSS/JavaScript


GitKraken


Adobe Illustrator

Who wants pizza?


Everybody loves pizza... right? Well, hopefully because my partner Pocholo and I designed a website that's all about pizza. Glow In The Dough is not your average pizza spot, instead, we are heavily inspired by the neon aesthetics that have dominated the 1920s to 1960s.


Taking inspiration from neon aesthetics and our shared love for pizza, Pocholo and I set out to build a website where customers could browse our pizza catalogue, as well as place orders. Glow In The Dough sets out to create a wave in the existing pizza scene. Our goal was to introduce some fun back to the pizza scene. No longer is pizza all about stone ovens and heirloom tomatoes, rather, it is a place worthy of that Instagram photo.

Designing a style guide


To maintain consistency throughout our pages, Pocholo and I first set out to design a style guide. Within the style guide, we identified elements of our brand identity such as our mission and logo. Other important elements included text and branding colours.


After defining all of the native elements, we began building our interactive elements. These included in-text links, navigation links, buttons, text fields, as well as interactive images.


Finally, we combined some elements together to form our main navigation bar, input fields, as well as a rating system.

Creating a skeleton


With the style guide now complete, the next step was to plan out the skeleton of our website. This included how we wanted to lay out our information and how we plan out interactions to ensure that customers are able to use our website in a logical, predictable manner.


This stage consisted of planning out how every page will behave, most notably I was able to work on the about us, contact, cart, and checkout pages. I styled and wrote the code for a checkout form, making sure to include all of the necessary information required.

Creating assets


As we began finalizing our project, Pocholo and I wanted to jazz things up with some promotions for our new website. Using Adobe Illustrator, I was able to create three promotional banners that we could fit into the carousel in our landing page.


Final touches


The final touches we made to our project was to include all the proper assets. Because neither Pocholo nor myself are experts at making pizzas, we turned to AI to help generate our thumbnails. In addition, we also added a system for customers to rate our pizzas, find our location by embedding a map, as well as adding simple JavaScript to notify when an order has been placed.
