Objective


Enhance TERAs BEAN's in-store customer interactions to increase overall customer engagement by designing and developing solutions.


Understanding The Problem


TERAs BEAN, an independent coffee shop, faces the challenge of competing against large corporations, with a primary focus on catering to university students. The goal is to create a welcoming, engaging space for all customers.

Gathering Insights


To gain insights into customer interactions at TERAs BEAN, our team, including Matthew, Alexandra, and myself, conducted on-site observations. We noticed that many customers, mostly university students, used the space for studying or work. Unfortunately, some potential customers walked in and out without ordering due to a small and easily overlooked menu and the coffee shop's limited online presence.

User Ethnography


Collaborating with Noah, Matthew, and Jasper, we created a user journey map to visualize the typical TERAs BEAN experience for a student like Noah. This mapping process identified pain points and positive interactions, informing our solution.

User Participatory Workshop


Our team organized a participatory workshop with SFU students to uncover their needs and frustrations when studying or working at cafes. The workshop consisted of four components: initial screening, moodboard creation, study space tier ranking, and speed ideation.


Our findings highlighted key preferences, including the importance of seating variety, access to power outlets, good hygiene, and ample natural lighting. Students disliked cubicles and areas with heavy foot traffic, as well as distractions such as loud music.


Research Compilation


I compiled and categorized all research data to identify user trends and pain points more effectively.

Our Solution


To address the identified pain points, we designed a table reservation system that reduces uncertainty when finding study spaces. The system allows users to choose areas based on their preferences, including environmental lighting, seating capacity, and access to natural light and power outlets.


Takeaways


User-centric approach. Listening to our users played a pivotal role in the project's success. User insights provided invaluable guidance throughout the design process.


Effective Communication. Clear communication with the client, TERAs BEAN, and other stakeholders was essential to align goals and build trust.


First Impressions. By making a positive first impression and demonstrating our commitment, TERAs BEAN trusted us to enhance their business.
