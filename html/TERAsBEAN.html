TERAs BEAN

Interaction Design

Overview

Objective


Research trends for TERAs BEAN in-store customer interactions to design and develop potential solutions to increase overall customer engagement.


Personal contributions


My contributions to this project included user-research, workshop development, as well as prototyping using Figma.

Initial User Research Summary


Research Goals: Identify potential pitfalls in TERAs BEAN's current business operation by observing how customers interact with the staff and physical space. Additionally, conduct user research to further strengthen hypothesis' to build a solution.


Research Findings: A large customer segment that is not being catered towards are students and customers who work at cafes. Additionally, TERAs BEAN does not feel inclusive for potential customers as the menu is hidden and menu items are poorly advertised.


The solution that we developed was an online seat booking system. This system would allow students, a large customer segment to book a seat online to better plan their studies. This alleviates some of the pain that can come with not knowing whether or not seating is available. Additionally, customers are better able to plan around their own needs such as outlets and potential noise factors.

Initial Concepts and Ideas


Concept 1: Digital Catalogue
Create a digital solution to help customers navigate the extensive catalogue at TERAs BEAN. The new TERAs BEAN digital catalogue will allow customers to order baked goods ahead of time, as well as inform customers about potential dietary restrictions.


Concept 2: Interactive Kiosk
A kiosk inside TERAs BEAN that would allow customers to explore the menu at their own pace.
The interactive kiosk experience will allow new and experienced customers to browse through the menu to avoid waiting in line.


Concept 3: Featured Menu
To encourage customers to explore the product offerings by TERAs Bean, a digital Featured Menu will be displayed outside of the cafe.
This interactive digital experience will entice and attract passerby's and indecisive customers to explore new drinks and products offerings and will increase foot traffic into the cafe.


Refined Concept: Online Booking
During our ethnographic study, an overlooked opportunity that was presented to us was related to the idea of cafe seating. This was also an opportunity we had previously overlooked during the creation of our design concepts.
This meant that this design solution was within the scope of our research.

User Testing


As a team, we created a workshop to collect data from university students to better understand what their study habits and preferences were. What we included in this workshop were a short questionnaire, a brainstorm collage, study space tier list, as well as speed ideating.


What we found was that users tend to prefer natural lighting, and white light for the interior lights. Additionally, different seating options are highly valued as users noted that they rarely study alone.


Gathering all of the collected data, I sorted the information into data that would be relevant to our design solution. This included data on what was already done at TERAs BEAN that is already working, what could change, as well as user trends. Additionally, I arranged the brainstorm into must-have and nice-to-have categories. The arrangement considered physical features of workspaces.

Final Design


Using the data that I organized, Matthew and Noah designed a tablet and desktop interface. The desktop handled the online reservations on the user side, while the tablet was the in-house system to notify customers whether or not a table was reserved or free.
