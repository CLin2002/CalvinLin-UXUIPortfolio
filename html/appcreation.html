Big ED

UI Design

Overview

Objective


Big ED is a charitable organization that aims to suppot education worldwide. Our goal is to encourage more people to donate to charitable causes by offering convenience and transparency throughout our app.


Personal contributions


My contributions to this project included researching charitable organizations, conducting usability tests (think-aloud/AB testing), designing the concept, as well as prototyping using Figma.

Initial Research Summary


Research Goals: To develop an understanding of the domain of charitable donations, identify user behaviours and potential high friction areas that prevent users from donating.


Research Findings: During the initial research phase, my group and I determined factors that discouraged potential donors. Most notably, lack of transparency, the feeling of their contributions being insignificant, as well as an overwhelming amount of choices make potential donors anxious about making their first donation.


Our proposed app will solve the listed issues by offering extensive breakdowns of how donations are used, quantifying community donations to highlight impact, as well as specifically tailor towards a niche so users have an easy time understanding what they are donating to. Based on these solutions, as a team we developed a primary and secondary user persona and I designed our experience maps.



















Initial Concepts and Ideas


Concept 1: Pen-pal donation
A pen-pal donation system allows donors to donate to specific individuals from other parts of the world. Donors can connect with recipients one on one and develop a stronger connection with one another.


Concept 2: Direct pick-up
A direct pick-up system aims to enable donors to quickly and easily request a donation pick-up directly from their doorstep, without the need of traveling to physical locations to drop-off donations.


Concept 3: Emergency Relief
Discover and connect with emergency relief requests put out by registered organizations and governments.


Refined Concept:
A charity organization that focuses on supporting all levels of education for students worldwide. Achieve this by offering different campaigns, donation transparency and convenience for donors.

Introducing BigED


Our educational donation organization is named BigED. BigED's priority is to promote campaigns surrounding education worldwide. During the initial ideation phase, I designed our logo using Procreate and Adobe Illustrator.

Refinement and style guide


As we moved forward, to maintain brand language and consistency, we developed our own style guide. This consisted of our colour palette, type, as well as interactive elements. Using the style guide, we maintained a high level of standard and consistency throughout all of our pages. My personal contributions included working with our type and brand colours. At this stage, I was able to further refine the Donations page to better represent BigED.


Interactive Prototype


Our first completed mockup and prototype included Onboarding, as well as News, Discover, Profile, and Donations pages. The first completed mockup included all necessary screens to complete donations such as overlays for payment, the ability to choose different donation methods, as well as the ability to track item donations. Using Figma, I designed the Donations page, including all of the appropriate interactions.


Usability testing


During usability testing, I was able to gather valuable insights from my interview. First was that, despite believing that features such as live-tracking of donations was a valuable feature, users noted that it was not essential to their overall experience. Secondly, users found the subscription and history page to be too similar. There were some other hierarchy issues as well, such as important text that is easy to miss.


I sorted all of the data gathered by my team, with important insights under its respective sub-page in our app. Breaking down concerns and issues with our first iteration, the entire team brainstormed potential solutions and opportunities that could be implemented in our final prototype.

Final Design


With new insights in mind, I began implementing changes to the Donations page. This included merging the Subscription and History page, making it an extensive list of all active and past donations, as well as correcting issues in hierarchy within the Tracking page.


The major changes made to the Donations page was to combine both subscription and history into a centralized Donation tab. The combination of the tabs was to help centralize information, making the overall user-experience more postiive. In addition, limiting the amount of information offered helped reduce the cognitive load put upon the users in the previous iteration.

Product Page


As my team and I approached our final milestone, one decision we made was to create a short product page. This helped assist us in presenting our process and finished product. I was responsible for creating the website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
