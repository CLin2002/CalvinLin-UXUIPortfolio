Objective


Big ED is a charity app focusing on education development, a project designed and executed by my team and me. Our goal was to address the challenges many new users encounter when deciding how to make donations. The app's premise is simple: it helps users choose a cause to support while providing all the necessary information for informed decisions. Our mission was to remove the barriers that deter users from contributing to those in need.


Understanding The Problem


Making your first donation can be a daunting experience. Users often face a multitude of options and must conduct extensive research to find the charity that aligns with their values and goals. Big ED was created to alleviate the stress associated with charitable giving, catering to both new and experienced donors.

Gathering Insights


In our initial research, we sought to understand why users hesitated to donate. We discovered that 60% of young adults believed seeing the direct impact of their donations would significantly influence their giving decisions. We learned that too many options can overwhelm users and reduce successful donations.

Q: What is preventing users from making their first donation?

" When the number of available options is small, these costs are negligible, but the costs grow with the number of options. Eventually, each new option makes us feel worse off than we did before. "


-Barry Schwartz (Professor of Psychology at Swarthmore College in Pennsylvania)

Framing The Approach


Through these insights, a follow-up interview with 6 inexperienced donors was conducted by the team to understand the behaviours surrounding philanthropy. From our research it became evident that the issues potential donors faced were the fact that one-time donors did not feel that their contributions were significant as they could not correlate their donations to a tangible goal.

This greatly influenced our framing of “How might we help a potential donor understand the impacts of their donation?”

Introducing Big ED


Within Big ED’s app interface, new users can expect transparent campaigns that highlight how every contribution impacts their community. Additionally, users will be able to track their physical donations from start to finish. By being transparent, users will clearly see the impact of their donations, empowering potential donors to create their first donation, allowing them to easily research campaigns at their own pace.

Throughout the project, I was mainly responsible for creating the Donation Tracking page, brand logo, as well as conduct user research.


Usability testing


To ensure the usability and effectiveness of the Big ED charity app, we conducted rigorous usability testing, employing a combination of the think-aloud method and A/B testing. This approach allowed us to gain valuable insights into user interactions and preferences.


Think-Aloud Method


During the think-aloud sessions, users were asked to vocalize their thoughts and actions while using the app. This method provided real-time feedback on their experiences and helped us identify user pain points and areas for improvement.


A/B Testing


In addition to think-aloud sessions, we conducted A/B testing to assess the impact of specific design elements, particularly the use of color within the system. Users were presented with two versions of certain screens, and their preferences and feedback were analyzed.


Key Findings


From our usability testing efforts, we uncovered several critical issues and insights:

Transparency of Supporters:

" I can see how many people are contributing towards a campaign, but it doesn't help my understanding if I can't visually see how impactful the donations have been. "

A/B Testing Insights


During A/B tests, users provided valuable feedback on the use of color within the app. While overall color did not significantly impact the user experience, users expressed a preference for yellow buttons related to monetary functions. Yellow was associated more strongly with money in users' minds compared to the color blue.


Iterative Design: Armed with these insights, we prioritized design improvements to address these issues in subsequent iterations. By enhancing transparency, optimizing screen size and interactivity, and refining the donation tracking system, we aimed to create a more user-friendly and intuitive app.

Compilation of research findings

Final Mockup


Incorporating user feedback from usability testing, I made critical adjustments to improve the user experience. The donation tracking was separated into active and completed donations, offering users clear insights into their donation status. The discovery page introduced a drop-down filter for enhanced usability. The cards on the discovery page were redesigned to be more linear, accommodating larger thumbnail images and more campaign information.

Takeaways


Quality over quantity. Users value quality campaigns that make a meaningful impact more than being overwhelmed by numerous options. Prioritizing user needs allowed us to create an outstanding user experience and product.


Respect what users’ say. Our research findings were invaluable to our project's success. Listening to users' feedback was crucial, and we considered it carefully throughout the design process.


Let users’ make their own decisions. Big ED's primary objective was to provide users with a positive experience and empower them to make informed decisions. We offered complete campaign breakdowns, allowing users to decide whether to donate.
