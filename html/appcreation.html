Overview


Big ED is a charity app focused on education development designed by me and my team to overcome the challenges many new users face when deciding how to make donations. The premise is simple: choose a cause to support and be presented with all the information needed to make an informed decision. Our goal was to remove the stresses that cause users to shy away from helping those in need.


Understanding The Problem


Making your first donation can be daunting. Users are bombarded by options and need to perform extensive research in order to find the charity that best suits their needs and goals. Big ED alleviates the stresses associated with donating, supporting both new and experienced users in their donation goals.

Our goals were to:

  • Make donations easy
  • Create an solution for recurring donors
  • Design a platform that presents users with all the necessary information

Gathering Insights


During our initial research, we wanted to identify why users were hesitant to donate. We found that 60% of young adults believed that seeing the impact of their donations would significantly affect their decision to give. Additionally, famous study by Columbia University in a , researchers set out jams for customers to sample. Every few hours, they would switch from 24 jams to just six jams. When there were 24 jams, 60% of customers got a sample, but only 3% bought a jar. When there are only six jams, only 40% of customers would get a sample, but 30% bought a jar.



Users who could not directly see their contributions making a difference often experienced a disconnect that deters them from making donations. Bombarding users with an overwhelming amount of options also led to fewer successful donations. It became the mission of Big ED to solve these problems in order to create a welcoming platform for donors.

Wireframing The Solution


Based on the problems that we have identified, I worked towards addressing these pains by working on a donation tracking page, as well as co-design the discovery page with Jasper. The goal of the tracking page was to allow users to track their donations from start to finish. Transparency in the discovery page was a necessity, giving users the power to do all of the necessary research on a campaign.


I quickly began mocking up some basic wireframes to gain further insights from the teaching team on the overall structure of these pages. This involved establishing a content hierarchy and layout for future iterations.

Developing A Brand Identity


As a team, we developed a style guide for Big ED. I designed and created our brand logo using Figma to further enhance our brand identity. Using our style guide, we got to work on creating our interactive mockups to prepare for user testing.


Usability testing


Through user testing using the think aloud method and A/B testing, the most pressing issues we found were that the discovery page cards could use more transparent methods of identifying how many supporters are backing a campaign. Users noted that the discovery page filter and cards were too small and hard to interact with and read. Additionally, users noted that the donation tracking was confusing and lacking in connection with the rest of the system. Most notably, donation tracking did not indicate clearly enough whether a donation has been fulfilled. Some insights I gathered through A/B testing were that buttons related to monetary functions were better received when they were yellow, users noting that it is a colour more associated with money.


I contributed in sorting the data, with important insights under its respective sub-page in our app. Breaking down concerns and issues with our first iteration, the entire team brainstormed potential solutions and opportunities that could be implemented in our final prototype.

Final Mockup


Using the information we gathered during our usability tests, I was able to translate the feedback and implement them into our system. I split the donation tracking into active and completed donations to differentiate the status of donations. Additionally, the donation tracking became it’s own page that allowed users to see the status of their donations, as well as what they donated. The discovery page also adopted a drop-down filter that gave the filter a greater sense of visual hierarchy when being interacted with. The cards on the discovery page were also adjusted to be more linear, increasing the size of the thumbnail images and allowing more space for text and campaign information.
